GIS Laboratory Systematica Project: The Pedestrian Level of Service

The purpose of this project is to carry out an analysis on how friendly a city area is for walking, which is technicaly called Walkability. This concept is a critical concern for citizens since it has health, environmental and economic implications. In order to analyze a given Milan city area located around Bovisa Station, the Pedestrian Level of Service (PLOS) Model is implemented with field surveying and GIS techniques.

The PLOS Model is aimed at evaluating walking conditions on road and street corridors in urban environment. PLOS is a measure of comfort and safety of existing and planned walkways. It allows objective and sound evaluations of pedestriansâ€™ perception and response to roadway environment. Over the last years it has been widely acknowledged as a key and robust methodology in line with US standards of the road engineering industry. In fact the model is based on the proven research documented in the Highway Capacity Manual 2010, published by Transportation Research Boards of National Academies.

Once computed the PLOS on the area of interest by means of QGIS processing on self-collected data from field and data provided by Systematica, the PLOS analysis results were published in the Results Section of this Web. Additionally, in the Report Section there are specific details regarding the data collecting and processing.
